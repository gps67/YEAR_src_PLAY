ATabla - 13 century, also found in ...

bayan - metal bass - "Left"
dayan - wood higher pitch - "Right"

	both lean away, towards eachother
	turn shie to be under mid hand (L,R) = (ten-past, ten-to)

shie - disc
madaan - thin
kinare - edge over the wood rim

ghe
	left hand curved over shie
	lh.1 pulls in, taps tip
	_ghe_ is lh.2

ghin
	move left hand forward
	press with wrist
	lh.2 a bit stronger
	maybe bend the note early, but dont release it

ta
	rh.4 - resting in outer roll
	rh.3 - touching black disk, stays muting sound
	rh.2 - straight, lifting up in air
	rh.1 - taps kinare hard 

	na = ta - maybe not so sharp ??

tun
	rh.3 - not damping
	rh.2 - taps shie sideways, or rh.1
	ringing sound, but not too loud

dhun
	ta + ghin

ti
	rh.4 }
	rh.3 } shie
	rh.2 }

ti
	rh.2 - shie, esp dha-ti

t
	rh.1 - shie

ki
	lh.* - flat hand palm onto shie

ti t ki ti
	oomph on all 4, esp the last!
	A.K.A. "titkit"

tra ka
	like ti t except reversed order
	rh.1 - first (loud)
	rh.{2,3,4} - second (loud)

tin
	mostly like ta but:
	rh.1 flat mid finger taps madaan, worm motion,
	moves forward slightly, worm motion
	almost ring sound

dhin
	tin + ghin

titt
	mostly like ta but:
	rh.1 pad taps madaan and stays down

tak
	ta + ki - at same time, slight flam

----

tit 	= ti - t but written condensed
kit 	= ki - t but written condensed
titkit 	= ti t ki ti
titkit 	= ti t ki t  - I'm not sure

PALTA "variation"
TEHAI "three"
KAHERWA rhythm like a chaal
kali "empty" eg tata titkit titkit titkit ie ta instead of dha
home-run ie has the dhun
